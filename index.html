Protect Your Home: Indoor Air Quality

It’s strongly encouraged for all renters and home owners to check their indoor air quality.

Because radon levels can fluctuate, the EPA recommends testing when:
  • Living patterns change
  • You begin occupying a lower level
  • The last test is not recent (e.g., within two years)

You can obtain more radon education and a low-cost, DIY testing kit through the following resources:

Washtenaw County Health Department For county-level information. Michigan EGLE: "Michigan Indoor Radon Program" For state-wide resources. Michigan EGLE: "Protect Against Radon" For a quick guide to understand radon risks and how to test and reduce them. U.S. EPA's "A Citizen's Guide to Radon" The Guide to Protecting Yourself and Your Family from Radon.
